## Commitment Mechanisms

### 1. Lockâ€‘in
Sunk investments in **sticky (durable, specialized, untradeable) assets**. Boeing 747 example: after investing heavily, survival depended on making that strategy work.

### 2. Lockâ€‘out
Closing off alternative paths. Options not taken become unavailable â†’ opportunity costs mount.

### 3. Lags
Long lead times between choice and outcome. Must look deeply into the future, accounting for foregone options.

### 4. Inertia
Organizational resistance: routines, culture, political interests create persistence even without economic lockâ€‘in.

## Chapter Summaries

### Ch.2 Commitment (Priority 1)
- **Core definition**: Commitment as persistence driven by irreversibility
- **Arrow's insight**: If investment were reversible, firms could ignore the long term; with irreversibility, they must look deeply forward
- **Key concept**: Sticky assets (durable, specialized, untradeable)
- **Use**: Intro of Paper C, conceptual definition of AOC

### Ch.6 Flexibility (Priority 2)
- **Flexibility value** = value of being able to revise commitments as uncertainty unfolds
- **High value when**: Irreversibility + ambiguity are high, learning possibilities exist
- **Tools**: Staging, option-like investments, VC-style financing
- **Use**: Paper N's Promise Vendor model, "capital as options vs capital as concrete"

### Ch.3 Choice (Priority 3)
- Most commitment is concentrated in **few big bets**
- Focus on **commitment-intensive (strategic) choices**
- Use **costâ€“benefit analysis** vs principles/rules of thumb
- **Use**: Framing AOC and CR as explicit components in costâ€“benefit calculus

### Ch.5 Sustainability (Priority 4)
- Threats to scarcity value: **imitation, substitution, holdup, slack**
- Explicitly **game-theoretic**: competitors respond to your commitments
- **Use**: Positioning vs RBV and dynamic capabilities, especially in discussion

### Ch.7 Judgment (Priority 5)
- **Errors of omission** (passing up good commitments) vs **errors of commission** (making bad ones)
- Organizations bias this trade-off differently
- **Use**: Paper N's Analyst vs Believer investor heterogeneity
