# Option Value and Entry Timing

## Summary
Miller & Folta (2002) formalize the "foothold strategy" as a real option: small initial entry buys the right (not obligation) to expand later. Counter to NPV logic, **higher uncertainty makes delay more valuable**, justifying small bets over immediate full commitment.

## Research Question
When should firms delay full market entry in favor of small "foothold" investments, and how does uncertainty affect this decision?

## Key Concepts

### Foothold as Call Option
- **Option premium**: Initial small investment
- **Strike price**: Cost of full-scale entry
- **Underlying asset**: Uncertain market value
- **Expiration**: Window before competitive pre-emption

### Delay Premium
The additional value from waiting to learn, which increases with:
- Market uncertainty (Ïƒ)
- Time to resolution (T)
- Reversibility of full entry (salvage value)

## Main Results

### Uncertainty-Delay Relationship
Higher uncertainty â†’ Higher option value â†’ More attractive to maintain foothold rather than commit fully.

### NPV Undervaluation
Traditional NPV ignores the "time value" embedded in waiting, systematically undervaluing staged entry strategies.

> **Scott Stern Application**: This is the theoretical foundation for "Bayesian entrepreneurship"â€”small experiments buy information options that compound into strategic advantage.

> **Charlie Fine Application**: The operational implication is designing "modular" entry strategies where each stage is separable and expandable.

**Approach**: Analytical option pricing model adapted for strategic decisions
**Data**: Theoretical framework with comparative statics
