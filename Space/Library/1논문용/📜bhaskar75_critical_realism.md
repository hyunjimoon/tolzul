## ğŸ—„ï¸3: Transitive vs Intransitive Dimensions

| Dimension | Description | Examples | Implications |
|-----------|-------------|----------|--------------|
| **Transitive** | Socially produced knowledge (theories, models) | Scientific theories, measurements, paradigms | Fallible, revisable, historically conditioned |
| **Intransitive** | Objects of scientific inquiry (real structures) | Gravity, social structures, causal mechanisms | Exist independently of our knowledge of them |

**Key Insight**: Scientific progress involves refining *transitive* knowledge to better align with *intransitive* realities.
