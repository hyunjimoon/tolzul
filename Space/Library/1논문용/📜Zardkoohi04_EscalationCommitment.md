# Do Real Options Lead to Escalation of Commitment?

## Summary
Zardkoohi (2004) warns that real options reasoning can **backfire**: the very flexibility that makes options valuable can become a rationalization for endless continuation. "Keep the option alive" logic may delay necessary abandonment decisions.

## Research Question
Can real options thinking paradoxically increase escalation of commitment rather than facilitate rational abandonment?

## Key Concepts

### Option Logic as Rationalization
- "Uncertainty hasn't resolved yet" â†’ Keep investing
- "Upside still exists" â†’ Don't abandon
- Gradual escalation through small increments

### Escalation Conditions
1. High sunk cost visibility
2. Self-justification pressure
3. Ambiguous failure signals
4. Incremental investment structure

## Main Results

### The Paradox
Real options logic, designed to enable rational abandonment, can instead:
- Provide psychological cover for continuation bias
- Make "wait and see" always defensible
- Convert commitment to flexibility into flexibility to never commit

### Boundary Conditions
Escalation risk highest when:
- Individual (vs. team) decision making
- No pre-committed abandonment criteria
- Soft deadlines

## Critical Insights

> **Scott Stern Application**: Evidence-based learning requires **pre-committed kill criteria**, not just flexibilityâ€”otherwise "pivoting" becomes "perpetual piloting."

> **Charlie Fine Application**: Operational reviews need hard deadlines and external accountability, not just option value calculations.
