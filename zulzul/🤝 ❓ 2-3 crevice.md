[two coinflips vs three coinflips] is a special case, like three-body problem, that can be associated with [P vs NPC] (for brevity, use NPC to note NP\P i.e. problem whose consistency certificate is absent). Reasons for this gap are that two is guarded by dual whereas three is attacked by the absence of consistency certificate. Engine for consistency certificate of two coinflips can be designed with isometry in dual: $X^*/M^{\perp} \cong M^*$)(from "the dual of a quotient space and a subspace" - Conway). This appeared from each core class: two-point construction and the fundamental theorem of linear algebra. In other words, P is reducible to two coinflips while NPC is reducible to three coinflips (nondegenerate).
