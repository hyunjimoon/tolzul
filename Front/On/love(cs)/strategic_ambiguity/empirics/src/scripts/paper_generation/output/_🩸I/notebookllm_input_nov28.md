# Act 1 (P1): Vagueness is an Option

## The Founder's State: ANXIOUS DREAMER
The founder doesn't know which future will arrive. They're anxious. But this anxiety is productive â€” it keeps options open.

## Core Question

## The Tesla vs Bosch Puzzle
- **Tesla (2003)**: "We're going to make electric cars desirable." No battery specs, no timeline, no unit economics. Raised $7.5M Series A. Now worth $800B.
- **Bosch (same year)**: "48V mild-hybrid systems, 15% efficiency gains, C-segment vehicles, Q2 2024, â‚¬850/unit, 2M units by 2026." Struggled to raise capital.

**Why does the vague one win?** Because **vagueness is a call option on the future**. Investors buy the option, not the plan.

## The Insight: It's a U-Shape
Survival probability looks like a smile, not a frown:
- **Extreme vagueness** â†’ Signals big vision, preserves pivot options
- **Extreme concreteness** â†’ Signals competence, credible execution
- **Middle ground** â†’ Signals nothing. Neither visionary nor operational. **Death zone.**

## When Does Vagueness Work?
**Modularity is the key moderator.**
- **Software** (high modularity): Pivot costs are low. Vagueness = valuable flexibility.
- **Hardware** (low modularity): Pivot costs are high. Vagueness = suspicious lack of plan.

## The Mechanism: Option on Information
Vagueness isn't just "keeping options open" â€” it's an **option on information**. By staying vague, you:
1. **Defer commitment** until uncertainty resolves
2. **Attract heterogeneous investors** with different beliefs
3. **Preserve the right to pivot** without explanation

# Act 3 (P3): FOMO is Optimal

## The Founder's State: RATIONAL DESIGNER
The survivor of Acts 1 and 2 has learned. They're no longer a naive dreamer or a trapped believer. They've become a **designer of uncertainty** â€” someone who calculates the optimal number of options to hold.

## The AV Industry Puzzle

- **LiDAR-first** (Waymo): High precision, high cost

- **Deliberately ambiguous** (Comma.ai): Maintains optionality

## The Newsvendor Reframe: Information Acquisition Cost

Think of strategic options not as newspaper inventory, but as **lottery tickets on information**:
- **Commitment Cost (C)**: Cost of locking into wrong path

- **Flexibility Cost (F)**: Cost of acquiring more information
