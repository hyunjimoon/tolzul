We present compelling evidence that increasing the frequency of online grocery shopping can significantly reduce household food waste for perishable categories such as bread and cheese. Specifically, a 10 percentage point increase in the share of grocery trips conducted online is estimated to reduce our food waste proxy by approximately 8.0% for bread and 7.4% for cheese. In contrast, we find smaller or no significant effect of online shopping frequency on less perishable categories such as cereal, cookies, and soup. These findings align with our theoretical prediction that the waste-reducing benefits of online grocery adoption are most pronounced for highly perishable goods, whose spoilage risk is more sensitive to shopping frequency and timing. These findings shed light on the possibility that a shift toward digital grocery channels can contribute to household waste reduction efforts, particularly for highly perishable goods.
