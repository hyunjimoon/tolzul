# EV Transition – Polycentric Governance Analysis

## 1️⃣ Overview
**Period:** 2019–2023  
**Core System:** Electric Vehicle charging infrastructure  
**Transition Goal:** Scale a reliable, interoperable charging network to support mass EV adoption.  
**Bayesian Frame:** Stakeholders iteratively updated beliefs about EV viability and infrastructure reliability as field evidence accumulated.

## 3️⃣ Polycentric Dynamics
- **Coordination gaps:** No single entity accountable for system reliability.  
- **Visible hands:** MIT MMI’s “virtual chief engineer” initiative; cross-OEM working groups.  
- **Naming / Framing:** “Uptime rate,” “reliability metric,” “99% availability.”  
- **Learning cycles:** Field failures → shared data → performance standards.

---

## 4️⃣ Evidence of Bayesian Learning
| Stage | Promise | Evidence | Update |
|-------|----------|-----------|---------|
| t₀ (Early) | “Fast EV adoption possible through rapid charger rollout.” | Frequent charger breakdowns. | Focus shifts to reliability over speed. |
| t₁ (Correction) | “We guarantee 99% uptime.” | Cross-network reliability audits. | Shared benchmark emerges. |
| t₂ (Refocus) | “Profitable, scalable charging ecosystems.” | Public-private partnerships succeed. | Common understanding of viable models. |

---

## 5️⃣ Quantitative Hooks
- **Funding trajectories:** Series A (2018–2020) → Series B (2021–2023).  
- **R&D proxies:** Government grants, infrastructure KPIs.  
- **Windows:** 2021-12 → 2023-12 (main), 2022-12 → 2024-12 (extended).

---

## 6️⃣ Comparative Insights
- **Commitment vs Flexibility:** Founders learned to promise reliability (commitment) while retaining technology flexibility.  
- **Coordination challenge:** Aligning public goals with private ROI.  
- **Effective visible hand:** Cross-sector consortia bridging policy and venture actors.  
- **Shared prior formation:** Consensus that reliability—not speed—is the key adoption driver.
