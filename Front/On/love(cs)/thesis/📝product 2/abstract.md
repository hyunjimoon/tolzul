Abstract. Problem definition: Food waste imposes significant environmental and societal consequences, with households responsible for approximately 37% of total food waste in the United States. While online grocery shopping becomes increasingly prevalent, its impact on household food waste remains an open question. This study provides the first empirical analysis of the causal relationship between online grocery shopping frequency and household food waste. Methodology/results: We first develop a conceptual model that captures householdsâ€™ grocery shopping behavior to motivate our empirical analysis. Using large-scale consumer panel data from 2017 to 2019, we analyze six product categories that vary in perishability. We construct a proxy defined as purchased quantity per inter-trip day per capita (QIC) to capture household food waste. To address endogeneity in online shopping behavior, we employ a two-stage regression with two instrumental variables: lagged household online shopping rate and the average online shopping rate among nearby households. Our results show that greater online shopping frequency significantly reduces household food waste for perishable products such as bread and cheese, while the effect is smaller or nonsignificant for less perishable products such as cereals and cookies, consistent with our theoretical predictions. Our findings are robust to alternative specifications, including the use of single-instrument and zip code fixed effects models. Managerial implications: Our results highlight online grocery platforms as a promising lever for reducing household food waste, offering actionable implications for both government agencies and retailers. Investing in online infrastructure and encouraging consumer migration to online channels may serve the dual objective of supporting sustainability goals while also meeting evolving shopper preferences. Importantly, our findings underscore the importance of incorporating product-level perishability into the design and targeting of digital grocery systems. Focusing operational efforts and promotional strategies on highly perishable categories may offer the greatest impact in curbing household food waste.

Key words: Food waste, Online grocery, Retail operations, Empirical operations management, Sustainable operations management
